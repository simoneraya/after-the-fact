02 About

A note on the author.

Born in Toronto, educated in Montreal, with work experience in New York and London — I am drawn to the intersection of geopolitics, media, and how stories reach people. I hold a dual MA/MSc in International History from Columbia University and the London School of Economics.

I currently produce research for one of the UK's largest podcasts and document the consequences of state repression on education in Russia for EU policymakers. My working interest is the quieter mechanics behind public narratives: whose logic gets called rational, whose evidence gets taken seriously, and how stories travel from archives and institutions into the culture at large.

I like the work of deconstructing accepted narratives and finding the more interesting one underneath. I move between critical thinking and creative expression — through writing, advocacy, and the occasional website. Outside of that: books, films, long walks, travel, and conversations that go longer than they need to.

This site is a portfolio of my research and professional work, and a home for the essays I write on my own time.
