Simone Geggie London · 2026

Born in Toronto, educated in Montreal, with work experience in New York and London — I am drawn to the intersection of geopolitics, media, and how stories reach people. I hold a dual MA/MSc in International History from Columbia University and the London School of Economics.

My working interest is the quieter mechanics behind public narratives: whose logic gets called rational, whose evidence gets taken seriously, and how stories travel from archives and institutions into the culture at large. I am currently working on a project for the Eastern Academic Alliance that documents the consequences of state repression on education in Russia for EU policymakers.

This site is a portfolio of my research and professional work, and a home for the essays I write on my own time.
