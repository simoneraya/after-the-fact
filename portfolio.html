MA / MSc Thesis 2024 — 2025

Carter, Camp David and the Mechanics of Peace

Dual MA/MSc in International History · Columbia University & LSE

A study of the Carter Administration's mediation of the Camp David Accords, built on three weeks of archival research at the Jimmy Carter Library, the American Jewish Committee and American Jewish Historical Society in New York, and the National Archives at College Park. The project traces how financial and military aid was explicitly leveraged as a mechanism to secure the durability of the peace agreement.

PhD Thesis Editing — Arnold J. Toynbee & Civilisational Historiography

Developmental editing on a PhD thesis situating Toynbee as a mondai hakken-gata — a "problem-finding type" intellectual — within 20th-century historiography, including his anti-Eurocentric framework and reception in Japan and the United States.
